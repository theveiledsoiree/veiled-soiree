The Evening

Not your average night out.

How to Dress

Masked Regency Formal

The theme is Bridgerton — think Season 2 ballroom, not a costume party. Pick a tab below.

👗
Ball Gowns
Floor-length, corseted, draped — any style that reads formal and intentional. Regency silhouettes are perfect but not required.
✨
Formal Attire
Elegant midi or maxi dresses, dressy jumpsuits — the standard is formal, not themed. If you'd wear it to a wedding, you're probably fine.
💎
Accessories
Opera gloves, jewelled headpieces, statement earrings. Stick to navy, gold, rose, ivory, or plum. The more deliberate, the better.
🎩
Tailcoats & Suits
A sharp dark suit, a tailcoat, or full Regency attire. Cravats and pocket squares welcome — this is the time to actually wear them.
👔
Colours
Midnight navy, deep black, rich burgundy, or forest green. Gold details earn points. No plain white shirts without a jacket.
🌹
The Details
Lapel pins, cuff links, a pocket square, dress shoes. The difference between dressed and overdressed is attitude — just commit.
🎭
Venetian Style
The classic — ornate, decorated, held on a stick or tied. Gold, black, white, or matching your outfit. This is the centrepiece of your look.
🪶
Feathered Masks
Dramatic, textured, full of presence. Feathers should complement your palette, not clash. Bigger is usually better.
⚠️
The Only Rule
You must arrive wearing a mask. Full stop. This isn't a suggestion — it's the one firm rule of the evening. No mask, you're not getting in.
🚫
No Mask · No Entry There is one rule that overrides everything else. Arrive masked. Keep it on for the opening. You'll know when it's time to take it off.
